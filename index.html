01 · Introduction

Setting the Scene

Starting January 5, 2025, New York City implemented a congestion pricing policy charging vehicles for trips to a designated Congestion Pricing Zone (CPZ). The zone covers Manhattan south of and including 60th Street, excluding select corridors such as the FDR Drive, West Side Highway/Route 9A, and the Hugh Carey Tunnel.

The policy aims to reduce congestion, improve mobility, and improve air quality across one of the world's densest urban environments. While other vehicles pay a flat $9 daily toll, taxis and for-hire vehicles face a per-trip charge structure.

Research Question

What is the causal effect of congestion pricing on taxis and for-hire vehicles' mobility patterns?

03 · Analytical Strategy

Difference-in-Differences Design

This study uses a Difference-in-Differences (DiD) approach to assess the impact of the CPZ policy on total trip volume. An origin-destination record is constructed at the trip level to analyze volumes for trips entering the CPZ (treated) and trips outside it (control).

The treated group comprises trips that start or end within the congestion zone. The control group consists of trips that remain entirely outside the zone. This design exploits the sharp geographic and temporal boundary of the policy to isolate its causal effect on trip patterns.

Pre-trend diagnostics will be used to test the parallel trends assumption, and event-study specifications will be estimated to trace the dynamic effect of the policy across weeks before and after implementation.

04 · Technical Pipeline

Production Cloud Analytics Workflow

01
AWS S3 + Boto3 Data Ingestion

Monthly TLC Parquet files (yellow, green, FHV, HVFHV) for Jan 2024 – Dec 2025 are downloaded via Boto3 and staged in an S3 bucket partitioned by service type and month. The TLC taxi zone lookup is staged alongside trip data to support spatial joins.

02
Databricks + Spark Data Transformation

Raw trip files are read into Spark DataFrames in Databricks notebooks. Each trip is joined to the taxi zone lookup to classify pickups and dropoffs as CPZ, Manhattan-above-60th, or outer-borough. An origin-destination trip-type flag is constructed, a post-treatment indicator (date ≥ 2025-01-05) is added, and trips are aggregated to the daily level.

03
Snowflake Analytical Querying

Curated aggregates are loaded into Snowflake for exploratory analysis and pre-trend checks using distributed SQL. This stage supports flexible querying across large volumes of aggregated data before causal modeling.

04
Python · statsmodels Causal Modeling

The aggregated daily series are pulled into Python to estimate the difference-in-differences specification using statsmodels. Parallel pre-trends are tested and robustness checks are run across all four service types.

05
Python · GitHub Pages Visualization & Delivery

Event-study plots, pre-trend diagnostics, and choropleth maps of affected zones are produced in the notebook and embedded in this GitHub Pages project website alongside the paper and code.
